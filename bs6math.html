Let's Learn Mathematics

Data:

Data is any raw unprocessed fact collected with the aim of adding to other data points in order to create an information. Example of data is the value obtained when your height or your weight is measured.

Information:

Information is the conclusion drawn when a set (or a group) of data or data points are analysed. An example of information is the conclusion you draw after checking your weight or taking your height over the course of some, say, one month.

Perhaps, you realised that you have gained some weight over the course of the month. You would perhaps make a decision to start exercising in order to halt the excessive weight gain.

You had to collect data first, in order to get the information to help you draw that conclusion.

Now, there are two kinds of data.

  • Discrete data
  • Continuous data.

Discrete data:

Discrete data is when the data collected take only finite number of values or whole numbers. Discrete data can be obtained by counting. Example is counting the number of students in a classroom. You would start from 1, then 2 and so on. When you are taking discrete data, you always count your normal 1, 2, 3.

Continuous data:

Continuous data is data that is obtained mostly by measuring. For example, weight and distances are obtained by measuring. Continuous data, unlike discrete data, do not have finite number values. They are normally decimals, and take a range of values.

Line Graph:

A line graph, also known as a line plot or a line chart, is a graph that uses lines to connect individual data points. A line graph displays quantitative values over a specific time interval.

The above picture gives an example of a line graph. Observe it critically to learn about the various attributes such as the axis, title and intervals.

Remember. You are to use the right tool for the right job, so make sure you draw your line graphs with a pencil and your ruler. Your title as well has to be written with your pencil.
