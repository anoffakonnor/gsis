Let's Learn Mathematics

Bearing gives the direction of a point in the cartesian plane from the origin, O. Remember, the cartesian plane is the plane in which we draw our x-axis and our y-axis, and the Origin is the point of intersection of the two perpendicular lines.

Imagine you are standing at the origin and facing the direction of the positive y-axis. In bearing, we can also say that you are facing North, normally indicated by the letter N. So the positive y-axis gives the direction, North.

What name do you think is given to the direction when you turn in an angle of 180 degrees, that is, when you are facing the negative y-axis? South. You guessed right.

Similarly, when you are facing North, and you turn in the direction of 90 degrees clockwise, you will now be facing East, which is the direction of the positive x-axis.

What about turning in the direction of 90 degrees anti-clockwise? Good. You will be facing West, which is also indicated by the letter W.

So we have North (N) for the positive y-axis, South (S) for the negative y-axis, East (E) for the positve x-axis and West (W) for the negative x-axis.

In this lesson, we will be learning about the Compass Bearing and the Three Figure Bearing.
